A deep dive into Japan’s intriguing electricity sidewalks

A deep dive into Japan’s intriguing electricity sidewalks



With the increasing awareness of renewable energy, solar panels have made their way into gaining popularity and an increased amount of installations. However, it is not the only way to renew energy. For instance, Japan has made a groundbreaking invention that is just as efficient, if not more than solar energy: electricity sidewalks. Let’s take a look at what actually goes on with these sidewalks.



The big idea is, people walk on these unique grounds and their footsteps get converted into electricity that helps power the city. Specifically, special materials are embedded in the ground that respond to pressure calculated using the walking person’s speed and weight, known as mechanical pressure. That pressure helps generate a small amount of electricity per unit, which then gets stored in the batteries of things that need power to be used, even WiFi! This generated electricity is called piezoelectricity. Normally, these special technologies are embedded in grounds that are most commonly used, especially in cities where lots of people walk around often. More people means more footsteps, which then means more generated electricity. Interesting invention, right?



This method of generating electricity tends to be much more beneficial in the long run for the environment. It can definitely reduce reliance on fossil fuels, the notorious culprits for causing environmental damage such as climate change and global warming. Plus, there is much versatility in integrating these electric sidewalks; they can be installed anywhere and nearly everywhere, thus maximizing the amount of electricity able to be generated using this method. Not only that, but piezoelectricity applications can last many years, making it useful for generating electricity in the long run for us consumers. So far, countries/areas that have begun incorporating these technologies include Japan (of course), France, the Netherlands, the UK, the US, and China. It is hopeful that this brilliant idea will spread to other countries as well with an increase of awareness about it.



Electric sidewalks are a genius way of generating electricity while reducing reliance on traditional (and environmentally harmful) energy generation methods through their piezoelectric technology. Its continuous incorporation will most definitely help the environment flourish again while providing us electricity benefits.
