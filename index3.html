Stores closing in the big '25

Stores closing in the big '25


Stores come, stores go. Because of these recent tariff wars and the fluctuating economies combined with many bankruptcies, many stores that used to be the highlight of the ‘90’s and 2000’s are now coming to a close. Here are 7 major stores that are closing down in 2025 in the US.

1. Joann Fabrics

Joann Fabrics is famous for its great quality and abundance of fabrics and yarn. However, this company is unfortunately closing down many of its stores all around the country. Reasons for its closing was due to the many debts and bankruptcies filed throughout the 2010’s decade, as well as its post-covid economic decline. Initially, Joann Fabrics was a booming company until it was bought by a private equity company, the culprits of its downfall. The new owners of the company supposedly have been making decisions prioritizing their profit over consumers, which was the major factor of that downfall.

2. Big Lots

The company Big Lots is known for selling high quality items such as furniture and clothing for a lower price. However, as of September 2024, a bankruptcy claim was filed and Big Lots failed to sell itself to Nexus Capital Management, a private equity firm, thus resorting to closing multiple stores around the country.

3. Macy’s

Macy’s, a good quality clothing retailer, is now shutting down its stores around the countries after realizing the increase of online shopping, basically prioritizing that over physical stores in order to save more money. This entire movement/decision is called a “Bold New Chapter” by Macy’s. 66 stores have closed as of this chapter.

4. Kohl’s

Because of a heavy downward sales decrease of 7.2% in 2024 and possibly an even heavier decrease in 2025, Kohl’s, another American retailer, is closing many of its stores. A notorious culprit for the downfall of Kohl’s is inflation, which is worsening with Trump’s tariff wars. Kohl’s also claims to be closing many stores earlier to avoid bankruptcies and save its financial tightness soon enough, as well as shift online due to consumer preferences.

5. JCPenney

JCPenney, a famous clothing store in the US, is set to close many stores in malls particularly due to ending lease agreements, meaning their time to lease an area in the places the stores are in is expiring. Like Macy’s and Kohl’s, JCPenney is also shifting its business online due to consumer preferences.

6. Party City

Party City, the go-to store for Halloween costumes and party accessories, is now unfortunately shutting down in many parts of the US. The reasons for it are increasing financial debts and inflation. To prevent any more financial debts, Party City plans to close more stores. However, another cause of those debts was the increasing competition between Party City and Walmart and Target, where more people shifted to those stores rather than Party City. This store was also known for selling wonderful balloons, but a helium shortage because of covid is quickly wiping those sales out, removing the soul of Party City from itself.

7. Walgreens

Founded in 1901, this pharmaceutical company is shutting down many of its stores country-wide. Filling prescriptions was not as big of an issue, but it still was. Walgreens was one of the businesses harmed by the opioid crisis (increase of illegal drugs basically) due to being accused of being affiliated in any opioid sales. Plus, more consumers, instead of buying gum or bandages or snacks from Walgreens, tend to go to other stores for those needs, which leads to Walgreens underperforming, and those underperforming stores (500 to be exact) now closing.
