Are solar panels a good idea?



As more nations develop, more access to electricity is needed due to the increasing populations. Fortunately, there are plenty of ways to increase the amount of electricity used, with one being solar panels. Solar panels, these technology plates installed on the roofs of houses and buildings, have been increasingly considered by more people. Their impressive ability to convert solar energy (the energy the Sun gives off) into electricity to power things has caught the attention of many. Although the idea of installing solar panels sounds intriguing, there is some doubt regarding the cost and aesthetics of this technology.



One complaint about investing in solar panels is that they can seem to be very expensive. Even though solar panels do cost $10,000-30,000 to install upfront, they are still better than a typical monthly electricity bill over time. Typically, most American households use about 900kWh per month on electricity. The average cost per kWh is around $0.15. To get the average monthly electricity bill, we multiply 900kWh with $0.15 to get around $135 per month. Now, solar panels usually last about 25-30 years. If we were to compare the hefty upfront cost of solar panel installments with the amount of money we pay over time for a traditional electricity bill, we’d see a crazy difference. If we have $135 per month spent on the traditional electricity bill and multiply it by (12(months per year)*25(years)), we would have spent around $40,500 over 25 years. That is about $10,500 over the most expensive upfront cost of solar panels. Although solar panels may seem expensive in the short run, they are very much cheaper in the long run.



Another rising doubt is that solar panels do not look aesthetically pleasing. While this may be an understandable point, solar panels are not designed for aesthetic purposes. They are there to do their job of significantly lowering your monthly expenses. In fact, with many people increasing their preferences for cost-effective solutions and renewable energy, having solar panels installed on your roof does not subtract from aesthetics; it instead shows that you care for the environment, which is a very good trait to have. Besides, these panels have significantly evolved to be able to have multiple color choices and look thinner and sleeker. They have become more aesthetically pleasing to the eye. Some panels may even look unnoticeable due to their ability to blend in with your roof. Therefore, aesthetics are not a concern; you should definitely invest in solar panels, they’re very useful for you and the environment.



Solar panels are here to help save the environment from further damage caused by fossil fuels and other pollutants. They are both cost-effective and aesthetically pleasing as well, making it a safe long-term investment for you.
